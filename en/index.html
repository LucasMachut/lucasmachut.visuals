PERCEPTION

Before booking, the guest is already forming an image of your place.

They browse Instagram. Open the website. Compare on Booking. Notice the light, the details, the spaces, the people who appear or don't appear.

Before reading anything, they already feel something.

My work starts there: building images that help your place be perceived with more clarity, more desire and more coherence.

VISUAL CONTENT

What I create

Productions designed to show the complete experience of the place, without losing the naturalness, atmosphere and real feeling of being there.

  • Hospitality photography

    Images of spaces, details, light, architecture, gastronomy and atmosphere.

  • Narrative videos

    Short films that tell an experience, not just a guided tour.

  • Reels and series for social media

    Content designed to create continuity, rhythm and presence on Instagram.

  • Content for website, Booking and campaigns

    Images adapted to the channels that directly influence the booking decision.

  • Production with human presence

    Real people, gestures and situations to make the place more alive and easier to imagine.

  • Drone and FPV

    Aerial or immersive images when they truly add something to the perception of the place.

  • Visual audit

    A professional reading of the current communication before producing new content.

REGARD

The Regard Method

A professional reading of your visual communication, designed to understand what your future guests perceive before booking.

Before producing new images, I observe what already exists: Instagram, website, Booking, photos, videos, reels, shown moments, absent moments, human presence, coherence across channels and the overall perception of the place.

The goal is not to criticize.
It's to understand what already works, what is missing and what may be preventing the right guest from feeling called by your place.

  • 01

    What the place shows

    Analysis of the images already in use: spaces, light, details, people, rhythm and moments of the day.

  • 02

    What the guest perceives

    Reading of the experience from the outside: desire, clarity, quality level, coherence and trust.

  • 03

    What needs to be revealed

    Concrete recommendations to better show the atmosphere, the real uses of the space and the continuity between Instagram, website and Booking.

PROCESS

The way of working

Production doesn't start when the camera turns on. First, it's necessary to understand what the place communicates, what doesn't yet appear and which images can make the experience clearer for those choosing where to stay.

  1. 1.

    Reading the place

    I observe the identity, the atmosphere, the strengths and the details that aren't yet being shown.

  2. 2.

    Visual direction

    I define a clear intention for the images: what they need to make people feel, understand and remember.

  3. 3.

    Capture plan

    I organise the important moments according to the light, the rhythm of the place and the real uses of the spaces.

  4. 4.

    Photo and video production

    I capture environments, details, gestures, transitions, human presence and key moments of the experience.

  5. 5.

    Channel organisation

    I think through the content for Instagram, website, Booking, campaigns and other guest touchpoints.

  6. 6.

    Final delivery

    I deliver clear, coherent and ready-to-use materials, with application guidance when needed.

VALUE PERCEPTION

The way a place appears changes the way it is chosen.

When visual communication is fragmented, the guest may not understand the true level of the experience. A good room can seem ordinary. A strong atmosphere can go unnoticed. A carefully tended place can seem less special than it really is.

Well-conceived images help the future guest imagine the stay more clearly. They build trust, increase the perception of value and make the choice more natural.

    Instagram, website and Booking need to tell the same story.

  • —

    Coherent content makes the experience easier to understand.

  • —

    A good image doesn't explain everything. It makes the place felt.
