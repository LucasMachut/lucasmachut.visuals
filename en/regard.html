What it is

The Visual Audit is a professional analysis of your visual communication — Instagram, Booking and website — done through the eyes of a European traveller who doesn't know you yet and is deciding where to book.

It's not a critique. It's a diagnosis.

In less than 48 hours, you receive a clear document with what's working, what's missing and what's making guests choose somewhere else.

01

Who it's for

You invest in your establishment. You care about the details, train the team, think about the guest experience. But when it comes to showing all of this online — something doesn't quite work.

Bookings aren't coming in at the rate you expected. Reviews are good, but the profile isn't growing. You've changed photographers, posted more, tried some reels — and you still feel that what appears on screen doesn't do justice to what people find when they arrive.

The problem is rarely where you're looking for it. In most cases, it's not the quality of the photos. It's not the posting frequency. It's a combination of visual signals that, together, prevent the right guest from feeling called by your place — before they've even read a single line of text.

That's exactly what the Regard Method analyses.

The method

The Regard Method

An analysis developed to answer one single question: what is preventing the right guest from choosing your establishment?

In 48 hours, you receive a complete diagnosis of your visual communication — what's working, what's missing, and where the unexplored opportunities are.

The result isn't a list of problems. It's clarity on what to do first, with concrete visual examples of what would be possible for your place.

Why this is different

I'm French, I live in Brazil, and I work specifically with hospitality.

This means I analyse your establishment with two perspectives at once: that of the European traveller who doesn't know you yet and is comparing options on Booking, and that of the professional who understands the Brazilian market and knows what makes a high-end guest book without asking the price.

Most photographers arrive with a camera. I arrive with questions. What's missing? What does the guest feel when they arrive at 8pm and there's no evening content? What does the absence of people in the photos communicate, even unintentionally?

This combination — external perspective, local experience, structured method — is what makes the Regard Method different from any generic analysis or social media checklist.
